Technology Analysis: Critical Limitations

Fig 2: The heavy, rigid grid infrastructure.

Engineering Bottlenecks

  • Too Heavy for City Shops:
    The massive grid (shown in Fig 2 on the right) needs reinforced floors. It is too heavy to fit in normal city shops (the smaller formats on the left).
  • Clumsy Robots:
    Robots crush soft fruit. Humans are still needed, killing profit margins.
  • High Energy Cost:
    The centralized hive runs hot. It needs industrial cooling not viable in micro-centers.

SCENARIO 1

The "Giant Warehouse" Trap

The Strategy: Doubling down on "Giga-Factories".

  • The "Useless Building" Risk:
    We pour concrete for 50 years, but the market changes every 5. If demand shifts to "Instant Delivery", these sheds become empty monuments.
  • High Risk of Failure:
    A "Giga-Hive" is a single point of failure. One fire or outage paralyzes food supply for an entire region.
  • Critical Verdict:
    This is not innovation; it is Optimizing the Past. It ignores the reality of urban logistics.

Strategic Recommendations: The Pivot

Stop Building Giant Hubs

We must stop building cathedral-sized warehouses. The future is Modular, Distributed, and Intelligent.

  • Immediate Action:
    Halt all new CFC (Mega-Hive) construction. Redirect capital to "Ocado Zoom" (Micro-sites).
  • New Research Goal:
    Shift focus to AI Vision & Soft Hands. Teach robots to "see" and pick delicate food in messy, old shops.
  • New Business Plan:
    Sell "The Brain" (AI Software), not the building. License our AI to retailers so they can run their own local robot networks.

Summary
